Saltburn-by-the-Sea

Recommendations of what to do to make the most out of the weekend

Saltburn Cliff Tramway

Experience the UK’s oldest operating water-balanced cliff lift. It offers a nostalgic journey between the town and the beach, with panoramic views of the coastline.

Saltburn Pier

Stroll down the beautifully restored Victorian pier, the last remaining pleasure pier on the Northeast coast. It’s especially enchanting during sunset.

Saltburn Valley Gardens

Wander through the woodland trails of these carefully preserved gardens, home to a variety of flora, fauna, and the babbling Skelton Beck.

Surfing

With consistent waves, Saltburn has become a hub for surfers. Whether you’re a pro or a beginner, the town offers surf schools and rental facilities.

The Saltburn Miniature Railway

Enjoy a charming ride on this miniature steam train that takes you from Cat Nab station to Forest Halt, passing through the beautiful woodland of the Valley Gardens.

The Smugglers Heritage Centre

Discover Saltburn’s intriguing smuggling past. This museum, located in Old Saltburn, recounts tales of smugglers, ships, and contraband.

Hunt for the Saltburn Yarnbombers

This anonymous group decorates the pier and other parts of the town with thematic knitted creations. Each year there’s a new theme, turning parts of the town into a delightful treasure hunt.

ArtsBank

A five-story arts venue on the seafront, ArtsBank houses the works of over 100 local artists, ranging from paintings and sculptures to ceramics and textiles.

Walk the Cleveland Way

Starting (or ending) in Saltburn-by-the-Sea, this national trail offers stunning cliff-top views and stretches across the North York Moors National Park.

Enjoy Local Cuisine

Being a coastal town, Saltburn offers fresh seafood. There are numerous cafes, restaurants, and traditional fish & chip shops. Don’t forget to try the local delicacy, lemon tops – a unique ice cream treat!

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What time should guests arrive?

The bar will open at noon, with Jacob and his groomsmen welcoming you all in. Please can we ask that guests arrive no later than 12.30pm for the ceremony to begin at 1pm. If you are joining us for our evening celebrations, we hope to see you from 6pm onwards!

2. Is there parking at the wedding venue?

Yes, free parking is available.

3. Will the wedding be indoors or outdoors?

Weather permitting, the ceremony will take place outdoors. Rushpool Hall has quite extensive grounds, so you may want to go for a walk - bring a jacket!

4. Is there a dress code?

Formal attire please, the more colourful the better! You will be able to spot our wedding party in their lovely green outfits; if possible, please can you refrain from wearing similar colours to them.

5. Can I bring a plus one?

Unfortunately, due to budget and space limitations, we simply can’t afford for all of our lovely guests to bring a guest of their own. Therefore, we regretfully ask our guests to please not bring a plus one, unless they are specifically named on the invitation. Thank you so much for understanding!

6. Can I bring children?

With the exception of close family members' children, who are named on their invitations, we are hosting an adult-only wedding. Thank you for your understanding!

7. Can we take pictures during the ceremony?

Our lovely photographer Angela and videographer Gav will be around throughout the day capturing the magic, please can you refrain from taking your own photos during the ceremony (you’re more than welcome to take your own photos/videos afterwards!)

8. Can we post pictures on social media?

Please can we ask that no photos of the day be shared on social media until we ourselves are ready to post anything.
